The side advocating for strict laws to regulate LLMs is more convincing because the potential risks—such as misinformation, manipulation, privacy breaches, and societal biases—pose serious threats that cannot be adequately mitigated without enforceable regulations. These laws are crucial for ensuring transparency, accountability, and ethical standards, especially as LLMs become integral to vital sectors impacting public well-being. While concerns about overregulation hindering innovation are valid, they do not outweigh the importance of protective measures that prevent harm and uphold societal trust in these powerful technologies. Therefore, establishing strict, adaptable regulations is essential to harness the benefits of LLMs responsibly and ethically.
